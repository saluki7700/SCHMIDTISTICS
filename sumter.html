Record Three — Service

The Sumter Log

Richard Dean "Dick" Schmidt (1925–2005) went aboard the attack transport USS Sumter (APA-52) as a Fireman First Class in July 1944 and left her, an Electrician's Mate Second Class, the week she was decommissioned in March 1946. In between: five invasion beaches, a kamikaze strike close enough to change his ship's place in formation, and the long, slow trip home.

Service Record
01
Chicago, Illinois

Enlistment

13 Oct 1943 Service No. 726 95 54 USNR, V6

Richard enlisted in the Navy at Chicago, where he and his mother Nellie were living at the time. He reported aboard USS Sumter on 20 July 1944 as a Fireman First Class — engine-room work, the standard entry rate for a young reservist headed into the fleet.

02
Palau Islands

Angaur & Peleliu

15–17 Sep 1944 Rate: F1c Muster: 30 Sep 1944, Manus

Sumter landed advance troops at Angaur, then stood offshore as floating reserve for the 1st Marine Division's assault on Peleliu — among the costliest fighting of the Pacific war — before serving as a casualty-evacuation ship and sailing on to Manus.

03
Lingayen Gulf, Luzon

The Callaway kamikaze

8–27 Jan 1945 Rate: F1c → EM3c(T), effective 1 Jan 1945 Muster: 31 Dec 1944 & 24 Jan 1945

On 8 January a kamikaze struck the transport USS Callaway roughly 600 yards ahead of Sumter in the formation. Sumter took over as formation guide, and the next morning landed her assault troops — including Callaway's — on the Lingayen beaches. Richard was promoted to Electrician's Mate Third Class right around this operation.

04
Okinawa

Yontan Airfield

1 Apr 1945 Rate: EM3c(T) Muster: 31 Mar 1945, Ulithi

After staging at Ulithi, Sumter landed 1,352 Marines of the assault waves near Yontan Airfield on the first day of the Okinawa invasion — the last and largest amphibious operation of the war Richard's ship took part in.

05
Japan & the Philippines

Occupation duty

Sep–Nov 1945 Rate: EM3c(T) Muster: 1 Oct 1945, Matsuyama

After V-J Day, Sumter carried occupation troops to Wakayama and Matsuyama, Japan, then returned to Subic Bay to embark homebound Navy veterans for the trip back across the Pacific.

06
Mobile, Alabama

Decommissioning

19 Mar 1946 Rate: EM2c(T), effective 1 Jan 1946 Muster: 9 Mar 1946

Sumter arrived in Seattle in November 1945, where Richard was promoted to Electrician's Mate Second Class on New Year's Day, 1946. He mustered aboard for the last time on 9 March, ten days before the ship was decommissioned and he was transferred to the Receiving Barracks at Mobile for separation.

Vessel History — Keel to Scrapping

Sumter's story runs well past Richard's two and a half years aboard her — she was under construction before he enlisted, and spent twenty years as a working cargo ship after he mustered out for the last time. The five official battle-star campaigns below include two, Marshall Islands and Marianas, that predate his reporting aboard on 20 July 1944.

Battle Stars — Source Discrepancy

The family/NARA-derived record used to build this page credits Sumter with five battle stars for World War II service. Wikipedia and the Naval History and Heritage Command's DANFS entry both credit six. Not yet resolved — flagged here rather than silently picking one.

Worth Noting

As SS Gateway City, this same hull is credited by NavSource and Wikipedia as the first true container ship — converted in September 1957 for Pan-Atlantic Steamship Co. (the forerunner of Sea-Land), roughly a decade after Richard's Navy discharge. The ship he served on for the end of the war spent its second life helping invent an entirely different kind of shipping.
