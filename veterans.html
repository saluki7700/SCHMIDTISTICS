001
Civil War — U.S. Navy

Edward Alfred Henry Schmidt

The Family's Primary Military Record
b. 1833, Middlesex, England d. 1881, Kittery, Maine USS North Carolina, Southern Star, Sabine, Mahaska, William G. Putnam, Commodore Hull

The most fully documented service record in the family, with a complete narrative — enlistment, injuries aboard USS Sabine, a covert Dismal Swamp reconnaissance mission, and a wartime hospitalization for a mental collapse — already written up on the Schmidt Line. Not duplicated here.

002
Spanish-American War — Army

William Edward Schmidt

Enlisted Three Days After War Was Declared
b. 1871, Kittery, Maine d. 1959, Encinitas, California Co. D, 8th Mass. Vol. Infantry — enlisted Apr 28, 1898

Edward's son. Full narrative — training at Camp Thomas and Camp Lexington, occupation duty in Cuba, and his later life in Kansas and California — already written up on the Schmidt Line. Not duplicated here. His enlistment date is independently confirmed by a U.S. Veterans' Gravesites index entry dated April 28, 1898 — three days after the U.S. declared war on Spain (April 25).

003
WWII — U.S. Army

Malcolm Rene Schmidt

b. Aug 17, 1909, Washington, Kansas d. Sep 25, 2007, Chula Vista, California Enlistment record dated Feb 1, 1945, Fort MacArthur, San Pedro, CA

One of William Edward and Madge Schmidt's seven children (see Schmidt Line), Malcolm's WWII Army service is confirmed via a U.S. World War II Army Enlistment Records entry, processed through Fort MacArthur in San Pedro, California — consistent with the family's move to San Diego County in the late 1920s. No unit or theater of service recorded yet.

004
WWII — U.S. Army, Private

Donald Dean Schmidt

b. Jul 18, 1923, Washington, Kansas d. Aug 15, 1984, Garden Grove, California Enlisted Jan 1943, Los Angeles

The youngest of William Edward and Madge's seven children. A family note from the 1997 Schmidt Reunion records that Don enlisted as a private on January 20, 1943, in Los Angeles, and died sometime in the 1980s of lung cancer. His formal WWII Army Enlistment Records entry is dated January 27, 1943 — a week later than the reunion note's recollection, likely reflecting a gap between enlistment and processing rather than a real conflict.

005
WWII — U.S. Navy

Richard Dean Schmidt

The Bridge Between Three Lines
b. Dec 3, 1925, Washington, Kansas d. Feb 8, 2005, Peoria, Illinois USS Sumter (APA-52), enlisted Oct 13, 1943 – decommissioned Mar 19, 1946

Son of Kenneth Schmidt and Nellie Florence Bowland (see the Bowland Line), and later husband of Marylyn J. Seitzberg (see the Seitzberg Line). Richard enlisted in Chicago on October 13, 1943 and served aboard the attack transport USS Sumter (APA-52) through the war's end, rising from Fireman 1st Class to Electrician's Mate 2nd Class (Temporary). Ten muster roll entries place the Sumter — and Richard with it — at Angaur and Peleliu in the Palau campaign (Sep 1944), in Lingayen Gulf during the Luzon landings (Jan 1945), and off Okinawa at Yontan (Apr 1945), before occupation duty and the ship's decommissioning at Mobile, Alabama in March 1946. Full muster-by-muster detail, rank progression, and a voyage map are written up on the Sumter Log. Not duplicated here.

006
Connection Not Yet Mapped

Robert Thomas LaBarr

b. Feb 6, 1918, Peoria, Illinois d. Aug 28, 2002, Peoria, Illinois Service-adjacent record dated Mar 26, 1942

Confirmed as a veteran via a VA Death File record, with a March 1942 date suggestive of WWII-era service — branch not yet identified from the sources on hand. Robert married Geraldine Anna Barth in Peoria in 1946; his parents were William Eaton LaBarr and Mary A. Gardiner. He shares Peoria, Illinois with the Seitzberg family, but no direct link between the LaBarr and Schmidt/Bowland/Seitzberg lines has been established yet — included here because the service record surfaced in the same GEDCOM search, not because the family connection is confirmed.
