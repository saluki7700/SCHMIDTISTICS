Field Trip — Kittery, Maine

Kittery Itinerary

Draft / Placeholder — not final, open to input from the group

A working framework for the family visit — arrivals staggered through Thursday, departures split across Sunday and Monday. Research-heavy days sit in the middle where everyone's actually there.

Thursday Arrivals Travel Day
All Day

Staggered arrivals

No fixed plans — everyone lands, drives in, or checks in on their own schedule. Keep it loose since arrival times will vary across the group.

Evening

Welcome dinner, Kittery Point

A relaxed first meal once most of the group has arrived — seafood is the obvious move this close to the water. Worth booking ahead if the group is large.

Friday Full Day Research
Morning

Rice Public Library — Genealogy Room

Kittery's public library keeps a dedicated genealogy collection: cemetery directories, town vital records, and Everett Stackpole's 1903 town history. No staff genealogist on hand, so come with specific names and dates in mind.

Bring: Schmidt line research binder
Midday

Kittery Historical & Naval Museum

Covers the town from colonial settlement through shipbuilding and the lighthouses — including a searchable people/places database and an interactive timeline. Worth submitting a research request in advance if you want their help pulling records ahead of the visit.

Afternoon

First Parish Church, Kittery Point

One of the town's oldest congregations, with church records dating back to the 1700s that turn up in genealogical indexes for the area — worth a look if any Schmidt-line records touch Kittery Parish.

Saturday Full Day History & Shipyard
Morning

Kittery Point walk

A stroll through the historic homes and colonial-era architecture that give the point its character — good light for photos for the shipbuilding/history side of the site.

Midday

Fort McClary State Historic Site

A coastal fort with defenses dating back to the 1600s, overlooking the Piscataqua River — an easy, scenic stop between the point and the shipyard view.

Afternoon

Badger's Island & Portsmouth Naval Shipyard viewpoint

Badger's Island is where the USS Ranger — the first ship to carry a U.S. flag into battle — was built. The Portsmouth Naval Shipyard across the water was the first federal navy yard in the country. This is the natural photo stop for the shipbuilding side of Schmidtistics.

Evening

Family dinner

The one night everyone's still around — good spot for a shared meal before the group starts splitting off Sunday.

Sunday First Departures Flexible
Morning

Optional: Kittery outlets

Light, low-effort morning for anyone not racing to an airport — the outlet malls are right off I-95 and easy to fit in before a departure.

Afternoon

First group departs

Whoever's on the Sunday flights/drives peels off here.

Monday Final Departures Travel Day
Morning

Remaining family departs

Buffer morning for last-minute packing, a final coffee, or one more pass through the museum if anyone wants a second look at a record.
